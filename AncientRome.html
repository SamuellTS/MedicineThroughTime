Public Health

During this time Romans began to notice that bad smells, unclean drinking water, sewage and dirt made people become ill and in order to combat this they built aqueducts to carry clean water into their cities, public baths, toilets and sewers were all built in order to give Ancient Romans a way to clean themselves and the toilets were connected to sewers so all waste was removed from the city.

Beliefs and Treatments

Doctors during this time recommended more exercise, changes in diets or prescribed herbal medicines as opposites. Also during the this time doctors were quite expensive for most people so it was common for the head of the family to look after their household. Due to this they would use herbal remedies and common sense methods.

Surgery

Surgery at this point in time mainly consisted of bleeding. Internal operations were still rare at this point in time because of the risk of surgery. The other surgery that was conducted rarely was amputation with that addition of trephining which was where they would drill a hole into a patient's head in order to relieve pressure.
